title: FIR first steps
tags: fir,police,criminal,ipc,bnss,bns
content:
For an FIR, verify the police station, FIR number, sections invoked, incident date, accused details, complainant details, and whether immediate protective or bail-related steps are needed. Do not ignore police notices or arrest-related risks; contact legal aid or an advocate promptly when liberty is at stake. From July 2024, the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) for all procedural matters.

title: Legal notice response
tags: legal_notice,reply,deadline
content:
A legal notice usually states allegations, demands, and a reply deadline. Preserve proof of delivery, collect supporting documents, calculate deadlines from the actual date of service, and send a clear point-wise reply through a lawyer or authorized representative when the issue is serious. Do not admit liability casually in a reply.

title: Eviction notice triage
tags: eviction_notice,tenant,landlord,property
content:
For eviction matters, check tenancy proof, rent receipts, notice period, grounds for eviction, local rent control rules, and whether the notice was properly served. A tenant should document threats, utility disconnection warnings, payment records, and communication from the landlord before responding.

title: Contract review basics
tags: contract,agreement,obligations
content:
For contracts, focus on parties, effective date, payment obligations, termination rights, indemnity, penalties, dispute resolution, jurisdiction, and deadlines. Unclear obligations, one-sided penalties, and missing signatures should be verified before relying on the document.

title: RTI drafting basics
tags: rti,public_authority,information
content:
An RTI application should identify the public authority, ask specific information questions, include applicant details, and avoid asking for opinions or explanations. If information is denied or delayed, the applicant can consider the statutory first appeal process.

For employment agreements or termination-related papers, check salary, role, notice period, non-compete clauses, confidentiality, final settlement, and labour authority escalation options. Employees should preserve offer letters, salary slips, emails, appointment letters, and termination notices.

title: Summons handling
tags: summons,court,appearance,deadline
content:
A summons requires careful attention to court name, case number, appearance date, party role, and documents required. Missing an appearance can create procedural risk, so the recipient should verify authenticity and arrange legal representation or legal aid promptly.

title: BNS 2023 — New Criminal Law (replaces IPC)
tags: bns,ipc,criminal,fir,sections,new_laws
content:
The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 came into force on 1 July 2024, replacing the Indian Penal Code (IPC) 1860. All FIRs and criminal proceedings from July 2024 onwards reference BNS sections. Common equivalents: IPC Section 302 (murder) → BNS Section 103; IPC Section 420 (cheating) → BNS Section 318; IPC Section 376 (rape) → BNS Section 64. If a document references an old IPC section, check the BNS equivalent. BNS adds organised crime (Section 111), terrorism (Section 113), and enhanced victim compensation provisions.

title: BNSS 2023 — New Procedure Law (replaces CrPC)
tags: bnss,crpc,fir,bail,trial,custody,procedure
content:
The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) 2023 replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) 1973 from 1 July 2024. Key changes: police must produce an arrested person before a magistrate within 24 hours (Section 57 BNSS); bail provisions are in Chapter V; Section 479 BNSS allows undertrial prisoners who have served half the maximum sentence to get bail. Trial timelines are stricter and victims have greater rights to updates on investigation progress.

---
title: BSA 2023 — New Evidence Law (replaces Indian Evidence Act)
tags: bsa,evidence,court,proof,documents
content:
The Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) 2023 replaced the Indian Evidence Act 1872 from 1 July 2024. It modernises evidence rules for electronic records: electronic records and digital documents are admissible without special certification in many circumstances (Section 63 BSA). Secondary electronic evidence rules are clearer. Courts now accept printouts, digital messages, and electronic contracts more readily under the new framework.

title: BNS organised crime and terrorism sections
tags: bns,organised_crime,terrorism,criminal,sections
content:
BNS 2023 introduces specific sections not present in IPC: Section 111 covers organised crime (punishment up to life imprisonment or death); Section 113 covers terrorist acts; Section 69 covers sexual intercourse by deceitful means; Section 304 covers snatching (a new specific offence). These sections did not exist in IPC and reflect updated legislative intent.
